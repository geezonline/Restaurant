JOLLOF RICE
The origins of Jollof rice have been disputed all over Africa for years. From Nigeria to Ghana to the Senegalese Wollof tribe and even Liberia, everyone has a different tale about how Jollof rice came to be. While it’s close to impossible to determine who created the original recipe, what we can tell is who has the best one and by popular demand—Nigerian Jollof.
GRILLED CHICKED
Nigerian grilled chicken is believed to have originated with the hausa's who are known for their grilled suya meat in Northern Nigeria. It's a popular street food and side dish, often prepared by vendors who are traditionally from that region. The dish involves grilling marinated chicken (or other meats) on skewers over an open flame, a practice that dates back to nomadic herders. .
POTATO CHIPS
Potato chips are thin slices of potato that are typically fried, baked, or air-fried until they become crisp. They are a popular snack, appetizer, or side dish. They can be salted, unsalted, or seasoned with various flavors like barbecue, sour cream and onion, or cheddar cheese
EDIKANG IKONG
This soup is known as the pride and joy of the Efik- Ibibio people of Akwa Ibom State who created it. It is made with primarily pumpkin leaves and water leaves. The people of Cross River State in Southeastern Nigeria also treat this dish as one of their own but it is regarded as a meal from the Efik people.
